1. Generic Information

Generic Name: Lactulose

Brand Names (Pakistan): Duphalac®, Laevolac®, Gutlax®, Laktulax®

Drug Class: Osmotic laxative, Disaccharide derivative

Therapeutic Category: Laxative, Ammonia-lowering agent

2. Chemistry

Chemical Name: 4-O-β-D-Galactopyranosyl-D-fructofuranose

Molecular Formula: C12H22O11

Structure: Synthetic disaccharide (galactose + fructose)

Appearance: Colorless to pale yellow syrup

3. Pharmacokinetics

  • Absorption: Poorly absorbed in the GI tract (<3%).
  • Metabolism: Fermented by colonic bacteria into lactic acid, acetic acid, etc.
  • Excretion: Unchanged in feces.
  • Onset of Action: Laxative: 24–48h | Hepatic encephalopathy: 24–72h

4. Indications

  1. Constipation
  2. Hepatic Encephalopathy
  3. Portosystemic Shunt (PSS)

5. Mechanism of Action

  • Laxative: Osmotic retention of water due to fermentation in colon.
  • Encephalopathy: Acidification of colon → NH3 trapped as NH4+.

6. Storage Conditions

Parameter Requirements Remarks
Temperature 15–30°C (room temperature) Avoid freezing or excessive heat (>40°C).
Light Sensitivity Protect from direct light (store in amber/original opaque container). UV light may degrade lactulose over time.
Humidity Keep container tightly closed (hygroscopic; absorbs moisture). Prevents crystallization or microbial growth.
Packaging Store in airtight plastic/glass bottles. Prevents oxidation and contamination.
Shelf Life 2–3 years (unopened); 6 months after opening (if stored properly). Check expiry date; discard if discolored or contaminated.
Special Notes Do not refrigerate (may cause crystallization). Warm to room temperature and shake if crystals form.

Key Points

  1. Stability: Lactulose syrup is stable if stored as per guidelines.
  2. Freezing: Avoid – may alter viscosity or cause separation.
  3. Post-Opening: Label with opening date to track usage period.

8. Side Effects

  • Common: Flatulence, bloating, cramps, diarrhea
  • Rare: Electrolyte imbalance, nausea
  • Overdose: Dehydration, hypokalemia

9. Time-Action Curve

  • Onset: 24–48 hours (laxative), up to 72 hours (encephalopathy)
  • Peak: ~48 hours
  • Duration: Up to 72 hours
